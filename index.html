About Me

Hey! I'm Thomas Zechariah — a Computer Engineering student at Georgia Tech from Johns Creek, GA. I'm specializing in Computing Hardware & Emerging Architectures (CHEA) and Cybersecurity, two threads that reflect my passion for understanding systems at both the physical and logical level.

I grew up in a household that valued curiosity, hard work, and diverse perspectives. Being trilingual — fluent in English and Malayalam, and conversational in Spanish — has given me an appreciation for communicating across boundaries, both cultural and technical.

Outside of coursework, I'm part of HyTech Racing's Electrical Team, where I work hands-on with sensor transceivers and embedded systems on a real formula-style EV. I also tutor through Knack, because I genuinely enjoy helping others click with difficult concepts.

Whether it's soldering a PCB, writing assembly, debugging firmware, or building a web feature, I thrive in hands-on environments where the work is real and the stakes are clear.

I want to be someone who builds the machines that others build on top of.

To get there from where I am now, I've identified several concrete steps:

  • 1 Complete my CHEA and Cybersecurity threads at Georgia Tech with strong performance in advanced hardware and systems courses.
  • 2 Secure at least one research position or hardware-focused internship before graduation, ideally with exposure to chip design or embedded security.

  • 5 Build a network in the hardware industry through internships, conferences, and organizations aligned with my interests.
